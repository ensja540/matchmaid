# Google Business Profile — setup guide (Match Maid)

**What it does for us:** wins brand searches ("Match Maid"), puts a pin on Google
Maps, enables a knowledge panel, and lets us collect Google reviews.
**What it won't do:** guarantee ranking in the generic "cleaner near me" local pack —
that's for individual cleaners and Google can be strict with directories. Set it up
honestly as a *directory that connects households with cleaners*.

Manage it at **business.google.com** (sign in with the Match Maid Google account).

---

## Step 1 — Create the profile
1. Go to business.google.com → **Add your business** / **Manage now**.
2. **Business name:** `Match Maid` — the real name only. Do NOT add keywords like
   "Match Maid Cleaners Christchurch" — that breaks Google's naming rules and risks
   suspension.

## Step 2 — Category
- **Primary category:** `Cleaning service` (closest to how people search). If Google
  questions it during review, fall back to a more generic one like
  `Service establishment` or `Internet marketing service`.
- You can add secondary categories later.

## Step 3 — Location = service-area business (important)
- When asked "Do you have a location customers can visit?" → **No.**
- This makes it a **service-area business**, so no street address is shown publicly
  (you'll enter one only for verification; keep it hidden).
- **Service areas:** add your target suburbs first, then the wider cities:
  Riccarton, Fendalton, Ilam, Merivale, Addington, Christchurch, Auckland.

## Step 4 — Contact details
- **Website:** `https://matchmaid.co.nz`
- **Phone:** a real number you'll answer (optional but helps verification + trust).

## Step 5 — Verify
- Google will offer verification by **phone, email, video, or postcard**. Pick the
  fastest offered (often video for service-area businesses). You cannot appear on
  Maps until verified.

## Step 6 — Fill it out fully (ranking + trust)
- **Description** (paste-ready, ~600 chars):
  > Match Maid is a free directory that connects Christchurch and Auckland households
  > with independent, verified local house cleaners. Browse cleaners with their hourly
  > rates shown up front, check reviews and ID, criminal-check and insured badges, and
  > contact the one you choose directly — no bidding wars, no middleman, and always free
  > for households. Cleaners set their own rates and keep 100% of what they charge. Find
  > or list a cleaner at matchmaid.co.nz.
- **Hours:** if you want to look "always available," set it to open daily, or mark
  "Open 24 hours" (it's an online service). Otherwise set real support hours.
- **Photos:** upload the logo (`assets/logo-mark.svg` → export a PNG) as the profile
  photo and `og-image.png` as the cover. Add a few clean, bright images.
- **Services:** add items like "House cleaning", "Deep clean", "End of tenancy clean",
  "Regular clean" so they show on the profile.
- **Products / link:** set the main action button to your website.

## Step 7 — Reviews (this is the real ranking + trust driver)
- After a cleaner completes a job via Match Maid, ask the happy customer to leave a
  Google review of Match Maid. A steady trickle of genuine reviews does more for local
  visibility than anything else here.
- Grab your review short-link from the dashboard ("Ask for reviews") and drop it in
  follow-up messages/emails.

## After setup
- Add a **Google Post** every week or two (a launch note, a "now in [suburb]" update) —
  it keeps the profile active, which Google rewards.
- Repeat brand/citation consistency everywhere: same name, site, city on Facebook,
  Instagram, and any NZ directories. Consistent "NAP" (name/site/area) builds trust.
